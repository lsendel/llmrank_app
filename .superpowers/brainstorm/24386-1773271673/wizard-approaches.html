Wizard Architecture: How should the steps flow?

The crawl runs in the background while the user answers questions. Two approaches for orchestrating that.

A

Linear Steps with Background Crawl

Wizard stays linear (Step 1 → 2 → 3 → ...) but the crawl kicks off after Step 2 (Website) and runs silently in the background. Steps 3-6 are the new questions (Goals → Personas → Keywords → Competitors). A subtle progress bar at the top shows crawl status. When the user reaches Competitors, AI suggestions are ready.

Pros

  • Simple mental model — user just clicks "Next"
  • Reuses existing stepper component
  • Each step has clear validation before advancing
  • Crawl completion is invisible, no context-switching

Cons

  • If user is fast, they may reach Competitors before crawl finishes
  • 6 steps feels long — may increase drop-off
  • Steps 3-5 (Goals/Personas/Keywords) are lightweight — could feel padded
B

Split: Setup Phase → Discovery Phase

Two distinct phases. Setup (Profile → Website) triggers the crawl, then transitions to a Discovery phase — a single rich screen with collapsible cards for Goals, Personas, Keywords, and Competitors. User works through cards at their own pace. A crawl status indicator in the corner shows progress. Competitor card auto-populates with AI suggestions once crawl completes.

Pros

  • Feels modern and less "form-y" — more like a workspace
  • User controls their own pace — can skip ahead or revisit
  • Compact: 2 wizard steps + 1 discovery screen vs 6 linear steps
  • Competitor card gracefully handles crawl timing (locked → ready)

Cons

  • More complex UI — accordion/card state management
  • Non-linear means some users might skip important inputs
  • Harder to validate — can't enforce "complete this before that"
  • Bigger departure from current stepper-based wizard

Recommendation: A (Linear Steps)

The existing wizard is already stepper-based — extending it with 3-4 new steps is the lowest-risk path. The "long wizard" concern is mitigated because Steps 3-5 are quick single-question screens (not forms), so they feel fast. And we can collapse the stepper to show phases ("Setup" / "Discovery" / "Launch") instead of individual step numbers, so it doesn't look overwhelming.
